General Information

The IBM Bobathon is a full-day, in-person learning event where students experience IBM Bob through practical labs, build challenges, and career development. Over two days (July 15 and July 16), each day runs from morning through the afternoon — covering hands-on AI labs in the morning and a career panel + resume roast in the afternoon.

Dates: July 15 and 16, 2026 — choose one day to attend

Location: IBM Toronto, 16 York Street

Format: Full-day event (morning lab + afternoon career session)

  • Day 1 — July 15: Doors open 8:30 AM
  • Day 2 — July 16: Doors open 8:30 AM

Register for your preferred day (Day 1: July 15 or Day 2: July 16). You'll receive confirmation details two weeks before the event.

The IBM Bobathon is open to all university and college students. You don't need to be a computer science major - we welcome students from all disciplines who are interested in AI and technology.

There is no registration fee.

We will send an email to everyone who registered by two weeks before the event confirming whether you have a spot or have been placed on a waitlist. We will also confirm which day (July 15 or July 16) you are confirmed for.

The event is a full day split into two parts:

Morning — Hands-On Lab & Build Challenge:

  • Registration & Check-in: Doors open 8:30 AM
  • IBM Bob Introduction: Overview of IBM's AI coding assistant
  • Lab Overview + Build Challenge Briefing: Understand the challenge and what you'll build
  • Hands-On Lab + Build Challenge: Work through the exercises and add your own creative twist
  • Build Challenge Presentations: Showcase your work to judges and peers

Afternoon — Career Development:

  • Resume Roast: Get direct, honest feedback on your resume from IBM professionals
  • Career Panel: Hear from IBMers about their career paths, hiring, and what they look for in candidates
  • Networking: Connect with IBM professionals and fellow students

Technology & Tools

IBM Bob is an advanced AI coding assistant that helps developers build applications more efficiently. During the event, you'll learn how to use Bob for intelligent code suggestions, debugging assistance, and real-world application development.

No prior experience with IBM Bob is required! The event includes an introduction and guided lab. Basic programming knowledge is helpful but not mandatory - we welcome students from all backgrounds.

IBM Bob supports multiple programming languages including Python, JavaScript, Java, and more. You can choose the language you're most comfortable with.
