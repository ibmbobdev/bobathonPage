General Information

The IBM Bobathon is a 2-day student hackathon focused on building agentic AI solutions using IBM Bob and watsonx Orchestrate. Participants will work with cutting-edge AI technologies to solve real-world, industry-specific challenges.

Dates: July 15-16, 2026

Location: IBM office at 16 York

Duration: 2 days

The IBM Bobathon is open to all university and college students. You don't need to be a computer science major - we welcome students from all disciplines who are interested in AI and technology.

There is no registration fee

Technology & Tools

IBM Bob is an advanced AI coding assistant that helps developers build applications more efficiently. It provides intelligent code suggestions, debugging assistance, and integrates with modern development workflows.

watsonx Orchestrate is IBM's platform for building and deploying agentic AI solutions. It enables you to create AI agents that can automate complex workflows and make intelligent decisions.

No prior experience is required! We'll provide tutorials, documentation, and mentors to help you get started. However, basic programming knowledge is recommended.

IBM Bob and watsonx Orchestrate support multiple programming languages including Python, JavaScript, Java, and more. You can choose the language you're most comfortable with.

Challenges & Projects

Participants will build innovative solutions using IBM Bob for real-world challenges across three key focus sectors:

🏛 Government

Create solutions that improve public services, enhance citizen engagement, and streamline government operations using IBM Bob's AI capabilities to modernize legacy systems and automate workflows.

🛒 Retail

Transform the retail experience with intelligent applications that enhance customer engagement, optimize inventory, and personalize shopping experiences using Bob's advanced AI agent patterns.

🏦 Banking

Build secure banking solutions that enhance financial services, improve customer experiences, and modernize legacy banking systems using Bob's AI capabilities for fraud detection, automation, and personalization.

While we'll provide specific challenges, creative interpretations and innovative approaches are highly encouraged. Discuss your ideas with mentors during the event.
